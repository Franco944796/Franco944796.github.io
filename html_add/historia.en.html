1776
Declaration of Independence
On July 4, 1776, the thirteen colonies declared their independence from Great Britain, establishing the foundation of the United States of America.
1787
U.S. Constitution
The U.S. Constitution was signed, establishing the political organization of the country as a federal republic.
1861-1865
American Civil War
The Civil War was fought between the Northern states (Union) and the Southern states (Confederacy) over slavery and states' rights.
1929
Great Depression
The stock market crash led to the worst economic crisis in U.S. history, affecting millions of Americans worldwide.
1941-1945
World War II
The United States joined World War II after the attack on Pearl Harbor, playing a decisive role in the Allied victory.
1963-1968
Civil Rights Movement
Led by figures like Martin Luther King Jr., the movement fought to end racial segregation and discrimination against African Americans.
